Interactive experience reflection

Project: https://www.richbrown.info/

1. What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

I went with the "rich brown" webpage. The progress bar and floating letters that showed up as the page loaded were what initially drew my attention; these visual elements are uncommon on conventional websites. I was enthralled with the changed site's fluid cursor movement as soon as I arrived; I wanted to keep experimenting with it.

2. Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

I started by exploring its behavior by moving my cursor over the screen, and I saw that it had a smooth, delayed motion. I then tested whether other parts on the page were interactive by hovering over them, such as the "r" in the upper-left corner and the "b" in the lower-left corner. I then selected the plus symbol, which took me to a different area of the website. After that, I took some time to navigate around the layout and look at the background images and code-like writing. In order to further investigate the interactive effects, I eventually went back to the main page and kept dragging my pointer around.

3. What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of my time reading the author’s profile and clicking on links about him; after all, this website is dedicated to introducing a UI/UX designer who has worked on numerous graphic design and web design projects.

4. What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action is to move the mouse back and forth across this webpage. The cursor changes from its usual shape into a small dot, and there’s a slight delay in its movement, giving it the feel of a paintbrush. Scribbling back and forth like this helps relieve some of my stress.

5. What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

This website serves as a digital business card for a web designer. The designer has incorporated intricate details into every aspect of the site; the garbled text upon entry, the varying fonts used to describe his background, and the cursor that changes as soon as you enter all reflect this designer’s unique style.

6. How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

I see this website as a digital business card for this UX/UI designer. It features links to his various social media platforms, with text resembling code that outlines his portfolio and professional experience, and a scrolling background that displays his own videos. It effectively showcases his design style through text, images, videos, and the website’s layout.

7. What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (for how long and how many different times)

It is ideal to engage with this experience in brief, informal sessions as opposed to longer ones. The website's interactive features and visual design are likely to draw users in for a few minutes at a time. Additionally, it is appropriate for frequent visits since visitors may come back to explore the designer's work and relive the distinctive cursor effects. In general, the experience promotes short but frequent exchanges rather than sustained, ongoing connection.

8. How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The experience automatically communicates how to interact with it over time because of its open-ended and non-linear design. The lack of clear objectives, progression systems, or organized navigation suggests that the designers did not anticipate users staying on the site for long stretches of time. Instead, short, exploratory visits are encouraged by interactive features like playful cursors and clickable items strewn across the website. The idea of "casual and repeated engagement" rather than "continuous use" is reinforced by this design, which permits users to leave and return at any moment without missing crucial content.

9. What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience uses a variety of media formats, including as code-like text elements, interactive cursor effects, animated typography, and video backgrounds. Together, these digital media elements combine web design, motion graphics, and audiovisual narrative to create a hybrid interactive experience. A rich sensory experience is produced and user engagement is increased when motion, images, and interactivity are combined.
